About Georgian Wine Heritage

Georgia is the birthplace of wine, with an unbroken tradition of winemaking that dates back 8,000 years. Our unique Qvevri method, recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage, produces wines with distinctive character and complexity.

🏺

Qvevri Tradition

Ancient clay vessels buried underground for natural fermentation, creating unique amber wines.

🍇

525+ Grape Varieties

Georgia is home to over 525 indigenous grape varieties, more than any other country.

🌍

UNESCO Heritage

Georgian traditional Qvevri wine-making method is recognized by UNESCO.

Featured Georgian Wines

Saperavi

Full-bodied red wine with deep color and rich tannins from Kakheti region.

Rkatsiteli

Ancient white grape variety producing crisp, aromatic wines with citrus notes.

Kindzmarauli

Semi-sweet red wine from the slopes of the Caucasus Mountains.

Mtsvane

Green grape variety producing elegant white wines with floral aromas.

Kisi

Rare amber wine made in Qvevri with complex flavors and golden color.

Mukuzani

Dry red wine from specific micro-zone, aged in oak for exceptional quality.
